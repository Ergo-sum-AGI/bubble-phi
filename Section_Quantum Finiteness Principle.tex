\section{Quantum Finiteness Principle}

The anomalous dimension $\eta_* = 1 - \varphi^{-1}$ emerges from the requirement 
of finite quantum fluctuations. The free energy

\begin{equation}
F = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{n=1}^\infty \ln \lambda_n + \zeta(1 - \alpha + \eta)
\end{equation}

contains a zeta-function divergence when $1 - \alpha + \eta = 1$. With 
$\alpha = \varphi$, this occurs at $\eta = \varphi$. However, the specific choice

\begin{equation}
\eta_* = 1 - \varphi^{-1} \approx 0.381966
\end{equation}

yields $1 - \alpha + \eta_* = 2 - \varphi - \varphi^{-1} = 2 - \sqrt{5} \approx -0.236$,
\textit{exactly canceling} the divergence and ensuring finite quantum fluctuations.

This represents a \textbf{self-consistency condition} for quantum consciousness: 
the field dynamics must be free of ultraviolet divergences while maintaining 
critical scaling in the infrared.
